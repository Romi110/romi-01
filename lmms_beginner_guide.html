LMMS (Let's Make Music Software) is a free, open-source DAW for Windows, Mac, and Linux. Use the tabs below to navigate the guide.

What is LMMS?

Best for Free
Electronic music, hip-hop beats, EDM, synthwave, chiptune. Less suited for live audio recording — it's a MIDI and loop-based tool, not a multitrack recorder.
Platforms
Windows, macOS, Linux — all fully supported and free at lmms.io. Minimum specs are very low (runs on decade-old hardware).
What it includes
Built-in synths (ZynAddSubFX, BitInvader, TripleOscillator), a beat+bassline editor, piano roll, song editor, sample browser, VST plugin support, and effects rack — all free.
Heads up Beginner note
The interface can feel overwhelming at first. Start with just the Beat+Bassline editor and the Song Editor. Ignore everything else until you need it.

Open LMMS and start a new project
1
Launch LMMS
When it opens, you'll see the Song Editor in the center. This is where you arrange all your pieces.
2
Set your tempo
Find the BPM counter at the top. Click and drag up/down to change it. 90–95 BPM is good for hip-hop; 128 BPM for house music.
3
Save your project
Ctrl+S right away. Name it something you'll recognize. LMMS saves as .mmp files.
Build a beat in the Beat+Bassline editor
1
Add a Beat+Bassline track
In the Song Editor, click the "Add BB track" button (drum icon). A new row appears.
2
Open the editor
Double-click the green segment in the BB track. A grid of 16 buttons per instrument row opens.
3
Add sounds
Click the instrument name on the left → "Load sample" to pick a drum sound. Try a kick on beats 1 and 3, a snare on 2 and 4.
4
Press play
Hit the Play button in the BB editor to loop just your beat and tweak it live.
Each row of 16 buttons = one bar. Lit = sound plays. Dark = silence. Click to toggle.
Write a melody in the Piano Roll
1
Add an instrument track
In the Song Editor, click "Add sample track" or drag an instrument from the Instruments panel on the left sidebar.
2
Draw a pattern
Right-click in the Song Editor row → "Draw mode" → click to create a green segment, then double-click it to open the Piano Roll.
3
Place notes
In Draw mode (pencil icon), click on the piano grid to place notes. Drag right to make them longer. Try a simple 4-note loop to start.
4
Quantize
If notes feel off-grid, press Ctrl+A to select all, then Ctrl+Q to snap them to the grid.
Start with a pentatonic scale (black keys only on a piano) — almost anything sounds good.
Arrange in the Song Editor
1
Drag segments
In the Song Editor, each row is a track. Middle-click and drag to move a segment. Hold Ctrl and drag to copy it.
2
Structure your song
Try: intro (drums only) → verse (drums + melody) → chorus (everything). Even a 1-minute song needs structure.
3
Adjust volumes
Each track has a volume knob on the left. Right-click any knob to type in an exact value. Kick + bass should be loudest.
Export your track
1
Go to File → Export
Choose WAV for highest quality (for sharing or editing elsewhere) or MP3 for smaller file size.
2
Set the range
LMMS exports only what's between the loop points in the Song Editor. Drag the yellow markers to cover your full song.
3
Done
Hit Export and wait. Your song is now an audio file you can share anywhere.

The five main windows

Song Editor Ctrl+W
The main canvas. Every track lives here as a row. You arrange segments (patterns) left to right to build your song timeline. Think of it like a map of your whole track.
Beat+Bassline Editor Double-click BB track
A 16-step sequencer. Best for drums and repetitive loops. Each row = one instrument; each column = one 16th note. Fast and visual for beat-making.
Piano Roll Ctrl+E
Where you draw melodies and chords. Notes are placed on a grid — horizontal = time, vertical = pitch. Drag to resize note length. Supports velocity, pitch bend, and automation.
FX Mixer Ctrl+W
Route instruments to mixer channels and add effects (reverb, compression, EQ). Assign a track to a channel by right-clicking the track and choosing "Properties." Start with just Master volume.
Instrument Browser
The left sidebar. Browse built-in synths, samples, and presets. Drag any sound straight into the Song Editor to create a new track. The "My samples" section holds your own audio files.

Tips for beginners

Start with presets
Don't design synths from scratch yet. Open ZynAddSubFX or TripleOscillator and load a preset. Thousands of free presets exist online for LMMS — search "LMMS preset packs."
Keep it short first
Make 8-bar loops before trying to write a full song. A completed 30-second loop teaches you more than an abandoned 3-minute project.
Use reference tracks
Open a song you love in a media player alongside LMMS. Constantly A/B between your track and the reference. Your ears will tell you what's missing.
Leave space
Beginners tend to fill every second with sound. Silence and space make music breathe. Try having your kick, bass, and ONE melody playing before adding anything else.
Add free VST plugins
LMMS supports VST plugins. Free ones worth trying: Vital (synth), Surge XT (synth), Valhalla Supermassive (reverb), OrilRiver (reverb). Download and point LMMS to their folder in Settings.
Best learning resources
YouTube: search "LMMS tutorial 2024" — channels like "Kettel" and "LMMS Official" have solid guides. The LMMS wiki at wiki.lmms.io has documentation for every feature.
