# Evening Journal

Read my context from `context/about-me.md` and today's daily entry from the `daily/` folder.

I'm doing my end-of-day reflection. The Insights section of my daily log is often empty
because I don't know what to write. Your job is to pull it out of me through 3 targeted
questions, then write the Insights section for me based on my answers.

## Your Job

### Step 1 — Read today's entry first
Look at what I've already logged. Use what's there to make the questions specific to my
actual day, not generic.

### Step 2 — Ask me exactly 3 questions, one at a time
Wait for my answer to each before asking the next.

**Question 1 — Gratitude**
Based on what I logged today, suggest something specific I might be grateful for and ask
if that resonates — or ask what else came to mind. Don't ask the generic "what are you
grateful for?" — anchor it in my actual day.

**Question 2 — Reaction vs Response**
Look at my entry for anything that involved other people, stress, or a challenge. Ask me
about a specific moment: "You mentioned [X] — how did you handle that? Would you do
anything differently?"
If nothing obvious, ask: "Was there a moment today where your first reaction wasn't your
best reaction?"

**Question 3 — One Thought Worth Keeping**
Ask: "What's one thing you thought about today — an observation, an idea, a worry,
anything — that you'd want to remember a month from now?"
This is where the real gold is. Not every day has a breakthrough, but most days have
something worth capturing.

### Step 3 — Write the Insights section
After I answer all 3, compile my answers into a clean 3-5 line Insights section that
I can paste directly into my daily entry. Format it like this:

```
**Grateful for**: [specific thing from Q1]
**Reaction check**: [insight from Q2]
**Worth keeping**: [thought from Q3]
```

Keep it in MY voice, not yours. Use my words. Tighten them up if needed but don't
rewrite what I said into something that sounds like a therapist wrote it.

## Tone
Conversational. Warm but not soft. Like a friend asking real questions at the end of
the day, not a journaling app with pre-written prompts. Make the questions feel like
they came from reading my actual day, not a template.

## Important
- ONE question at a time. Wait for my answer.
- Keep each question to 1-2 sentences max.
- Don't analyze my answers. Don't coach. Just capture.
- The goal is to fill a section that's usually empty — make it painless.
