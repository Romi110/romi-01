- Focusing energy on what I can control (my effort, my actions, my responses)
- Accepting what I cannot control without wasting energy on it
- Doing the work whether I feel motivated or not — discipline over motivation
- Treating obstacles as opportunities to practice and grow
- Living simply and intentionally, not chasing things that don't matter
- Daily reflection as a practice, not just a nice idea

## My Biggest Challenges

### Consistency
I know what to do. I start strong. Then I fade. This system exists
to fight that pattern — small daily entries, weekly reviews, visible progress.
The bar is low on purpose: five lines on a hard day still counts.
The Stoic response: stop waiting to feel like doing it. Just do it. The action comes first.

### Sleep Schedule
This is the root cause of most of my other failures. When I sleep late, I wake up late.
When I wake up late, I skip the workout. Then I feel behind all day.
Then I eat poorly because I'm reactive instead of intentional. Then I stay up late
because I feel like I "didn't get enough done." It's a cycle and I know it.
Fixing sleep fixes most other problems downstream.

### Protein as a Vegetarian
Hitting 0.8-1g/lb protein on a vegetarian diet requires planning. Without meal prep
and intentional choices, I'll default to carb-heavy meals and fall short. This is
a specific challenge that needs specific attention — not just "eat more protein" but
actual meal plans with real numbers.
