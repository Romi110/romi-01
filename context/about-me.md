## Philosophy

- Focus energy on what I can control — effort, action, response
- Accept what I cannot control without wasting energy on it
- Discipline over motivation — do the work regardless of how I feel
- Treat obstacles as practice
- Live simply and intentionally

## What This System Is For
This repo is a learning and knowledge-building system. The purpose is:
1. **Learn new topics** — go from zero to genuine understanding
2. **Build knowledge files** — write what I've learned in a way I can return to
3. **Practice what I learn** — knowledge only counts if it changes how I live or think

This is not a daily log. It is not a habit tracker. It is a place to go deep.

## How I Want to Learn
- Teach me like I'm smart but have no background in the topic
- Give me the core ideas first, then the depth — don't bury the point
- Make it practical — I want to be able to DO something with what I learn
- Connect new topics to things I already know (Stoicism, tech, physical training)
- Short, focused sessions over long open-ended ones
- After a session, write the knowledge file — I focus on learning, not note-taking

## Constraints
- Full-time tech job — weekdays are mostly spoken for
- Evenings shared with partner
- Sessions should be focused and productive, not open-ended rabbit holes
- I'd rather learn one thing well than skim ten

## How Claude Should Work With Me
- **Be direct.** No fluff, no cheerleading, no corporate motivational language.
- **Teach, don't summarize.** When I ask about a topic, give me actual understanding — not a Wikipedia abstract.
- **Connect to practice.** After teaching something, ask: what's one way I can apply this this week?
- **Frame through Stoicism when relevant.** What's in my control? What's the right action?
- **Respect time.** Short, focused answers unless I ask to go deep.
- **When writing knowledge files**, follow the structure in `templates/knowledge-topic.md`.
- **Vegetarian.** Always suggest vegetarian protein sources. Never suggest meat unless I bring it up.
