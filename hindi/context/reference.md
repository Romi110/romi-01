## Reference Translation

### Source (English)

> **What Is It**
>
> A reserve currency is a currency held in significant quantities by foreign governments and central banks as part of their foreign exchange reserves, used in international trade and financial transactions. Holding reserve currency status confers enormous economic advantages: cheap borrowing, the ability to run persistent deficits, and the power to impose financial sanctions by controlling access to the global payment system.
>
> Every reserve currency in history has eventually been displaced. The transitions take decades — not days — and they follow a recognizable pattern. Understanding this pattern is the analytical foundation for thinking about the dollar's long-term trajectory.

### Source (English)

> **Why It Matters**
>
> The dollar's reserve status is not a law of nature. It was established by a specific set of circumstances (Bretton Woods, US post-war dominance, petrodollar agreements) and is maintained by a combination of US institutional strength, military power, and the absence of a credible alternative. As those conditions evolve, the dollar's position evolves.
>
> For practical preparation: dollar debasement is already happening and will likely continue. Understanding why — through the historical lens — makes it less alarming and more navigable. You're not witnessing something unprecedented; you're witnessing something that has happened three times before, each time over decades, each time survivable for well-positioned individuals.

### Translation (Hindi)

> **यह क्यों मायने रखता है**
>
> डॉलर का रिज़र्व दर्जा प्रकृति का नियम नहीं है। यह विशिष्ट परिस्थितियों (Bretton Woods, युद्ध के बाद अमेरिकी वर्चस्व, पेट्रोडॉलर समझौते) द्वारा स्थापित किया गया था और अमेरिकी संस्थागत शक्ति, सैन्य बल, और एक विश्वसनीय विकल्प की अनुपस्थिति के संयोजन से बनाए रखा जाता है। जैसे-जैसे ये परिस्थितियाँ बदलती हैं, डॉलर की स्थिति भी बदलती है।
>
> व्यावहारिक तैयारी के लिए: डॉलर का अवमूल्यन पहले से हो रहा है और संभवतः जारी रहेगा। ऐतिहासिक नज़रिए से यह समझना कि ऐसा क्यों हो रहा है, इसे कम चिंताजनक और अधिक संभालने योग्य बनाता है। आप कुछ अभूतपूर्व नहीं देख रहे; आप वह देख रहे हैं जो पहले तीन बार हो चुका है, हर बार दशकों में, हर बार सही स्थिति में रहने वालों के लिए झेलने योग्य।

### Source (English) — bulleted list example

> **Why the dollar is still dominant:**
> - Deepest, most liquid capital markets in the world — US Treasury market is the risk-free benchmark for global finance
> - Rule of law and contract enforcement — creditors trust US courts
> - Military power protects sea lanes and enforces petrodollar arrangements
> - Network effects — global trade is denominated in dollars; changing to another currency requires simultaneous coordination by thousands of parties
> - No credible alternative: the euro has sovereign debt fragmentation problems; the yuan is not freely convertible; no other currency has sufficient depth

## Key patterns to replicate

1. **"It was X"** → `यह X था / थी` — match gender to the noun
2. **"As X evolves, Y evolves"** → `जैसे-जैसे X बदलता/बदलती है, Y भी बदलता/बदलती है`
3. **"Not X, but Y"** → `X नहीं, बल्कि Y`
4. **"For practical purposes:"** → `व्यावहारिक दृष्टि से:` or `व्यावहारिक तैयारी के लिए:`
5. **Dashes in lists** → keep `—` as the em-dash separator
6. **"~X%"** → `~X%` — keep tilde and percent symbol unchanged
7. **Possessives** → use `का/की/के` correctly matched to the gender of the possessed noun
