# Freezer Meal Prep
*A 2–4 week food buffer using souper cubes — batch cook once, eat well for a month.*

---

## What Is It
Freezer prep is a different system from weekly fridge prep. Instead of cooking for the next 5 days, you're building a stockpile — a buffer of ready-to-reheat meals that covers you when Sunday prep didn't happen, the week went sideways, or you just want a no-effort dinner.

The souper cube system makes this practical: fill the trays, freeze solid, pop the portions into labeled freezer bags. Every cube is a standardized portion you can pull out individually.

## Why It Matters
A 2–4 week freezer buffer means food security at home. You're never more than 10 minutes from a real meal. It also makes Sunday prep lower stakes — you don't have to nail it every week if the freezer has your back.

---

## Your Souper Cube Sizes — What Each Is For

| Cube | Volume | Best Use |
|------|--------|----------|
| 2 oz | ~60 ml | Flavor bases, chutneys, tamarind paste, coconut milk, tomato paste, herb cubes |
| 1 cup | ~240 ml | Single servings of dal, curry, soup, chili — the workhorse |
| 2 cup | ~480 ml | Larger curry portions, grain batches, full meal components for two |

**Workflow:** Fill trays → freeze 4–6 hours until solid → pop out → store in labeled freezer bags. Return the empty trays for the next batch. Don't store food in the trays long-term.

**Label every bag:** name + date frozen. Frozen food all looks the same after a week.

## What Freezes Well

### Excellent — Freeze Without Hesitation
- **Dal** (all varieties: toor, moong, masoor, chana) — freezes perfectly, often tastes better reheated
- **Rajma** — one of the best freezer foods; the sauce deepens over time
- **Chole** — same as rajma
- **Soy chunk curry** — freezes and reheats very well
- **Lentil soup / dal soup** — any blended or broth-based lentil preparation
- **Vegetable curries** (spinach, cauliflower, peas) — most freeze well except watery ones
- **Palak paneer** — the spinach base freezes excellently; add fresh paneer when reheating for best texture, or freeze with paneer (texture softens slightly but still good)
- **Tomato-based sauces and masala bases** — freeze a batch of base curry sauce and build from it
- **Cooked chickpeas / rajma / black beans** — plain, no sauce, for flexible use
- **Soups** (lentil, vegetable, tomato, black bean) — all freeze extremely well
- **Cooked grains** (brown rice, quinoa) — freeze in 1-cup portions, reheat from frozen in 2 minutes
- **Overnight oat bases** — oats + chia + seeds (no liquid) freeze fine; add yogurt and milk fresh
- **Chana masala** — excellent freezer candidate
- **Smoothie packs** — portion banana + spinach + fruit into 2 oz or small bags, blend from frozen with liquid

### Freezes Well With Minor Notes
- **Tofu curries** — tofu texture becomes slightly more porous after freezing, which actually helps it absorb sauce better. Worth trying.
- **Paneer in curry** — paneer gets slightly rubbery when frozen. Acceptable for reheating in sauce, not ideal for eating plain. Freeze the sauce, add fresh paneer when reheating if you care about texture.
- **Eggs** — hard-boiled eggs don't freeze (rubbery whites). Scrambled eggs and omelettes freeze fine for 1 month.
- **Cooked pasta / noodles** — freezes ok, gets softer on reheat. Fine for soups, less ideal standalone.
- **Roti / chapati** — freeze with parchment between each. Reheat directly on tawa from frozen. Works well.
- **Paratha** — same as roti, arguably better reheated from frozen than day-old fridge.

### Do Not Freeze
- **Yogurt / raita** — separates and becomes watery. Keep fresh.
- **Fresh salads or greens** — texture destroyed.
- **Cucumber, raw onion, raw tomato** — cell structure breaks down.
- **Fried items** (pakora, samosa) — go soggy. Cook fresh or air-fry from refrigerated.
- **Cream-based sauces (heavy)** — may separate; use sparingly.
- **Potatoes in curry** — become mealy and grainy. Either remove before freezing or use in soups where texture matters less.

## Core Freezer Recipes

### Dal (Any Variety) — Freeze in 1-Cup Portions
**Yield:** ~8–10 cups from one large pot  
**Cube:** 1 cup  
**Keeps:** 3 months

Cook your standard dal — toor, masoor, or moong with standard tadka (cumin, mustard seeds, onion, tomato, turmeric, garam masala). Let cool completely. Pour into 1-cup trays, freeze, transfer to bags.

**Reheat:** Microwave 2–3 min covered, or stovetop with a splash of water. Add fresh tadka on top to revive it.

---

### Rajma or Chole — Freeze in 1-Cup or 2-Cup Portions
**Yield:** ~6–8 cups from one pot  
**Cube:** 1 cup (single meal) or 2 cup (meal for two)  
**Keeps:** 3 months

Cook fully — soaked + boiled beans, full masala, simmered until thick. Cool, portion, freeze.

**Reheat:** Stovetop with a splash of water. Add fresh ginger or a squeeze of lemon to brighten.

---

### Tomato-Onion Masala Base — Freeze in 2-oz Portions
**Yield:** ~20–24 cubes from one large batch  
**Cube:** 2 oz  
**Keeps:** 3 months  
**Why:** This is the single highest-leverage thing you can freeze. This base goes into almost every curry. Make a huge batch, freeze in 2 oz cubes. Building a curry becomes a 15-minute job.

Fry onions until deep golden → add ginger-garlic paste → cook until fragrant → add canned or fresh tomatoes + spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili) → cook down until thick and oil separates. Cool, fill 2 oz trays.

**Use:** Drop 2–4 cubes into a pan, add your protein (paneer, tofu, chickpeas, whatever), cook 10 minutes. Done.

---

### Palak (Spinach) Base — Freeze in 1-Cup Portions
**Yield:** ~6–8 cups  
**Cube:** 1 cup  
**Keeps:** 2 months

Blanch spinach + green chilli + garlic, blend smooth. Cook with a small tomato-onion base, season with garam masala and cumin. Cool and portion.

**Reheat:** Thaw, heat in pan, add fresh or frozen paneer cubes (or tofu), cook 5 minutes. Palak paneer in 10 minutes total.

---

### Black Bean or Mixed Legume Soup — Freeze in 1-Cup Portions
**Cube:** 1 cup  
**Keeps:** 3 months

Saute onion, garlic, cumin, chili → add black beans (canned or soaked/cooked), vegetable broth, canned tomatoes, salt. Simmer 20 min, partially blend for texture. Season with lime juice and smoked paprika.

**Reheat:** Microwave or stovetop. Top with yogurt or cheese if you have it.

---

### Vegetable and Lentil Soup (Big Batch) — Freeze in 1-Cup or 2-Cup
**Cube:** 1 cup or 2 cup  
**Keeps:** 3 months

Any combination: carrots, zucchini, spinach, tomatoes + red lentils + vegetable stock + spices. Blended or chunky. Highest effort-to-reward ratio of anything in this list — one large pot yields 12+ portions.

---

### Cooked Brown Rice or Quinoa — Freeze in 1-Cup Portions
**Cube:** 1 cup  
**Keeps:** 1 month

Cook normally, spread thin on a baking sheet to cool fast (prevents clumping), fill trays, freeze. Once frozen, break apart any clusters in the bag.

**Reheat:** Microwave with a damp paper towel on top, 2 minutes. Comes out perfect.

---

### Roti / Chapati — Stack and Freeze
**Keeps:** 1 month

Cook rotis slightly underdone. Cool completely, stack with parchment squares between each, seal in a freezer bag.

**Reheat:** Direct on tawa from frozen, 1–2 minutes per side. No thawing needed.

---

### Smoothie Packs — 2-oz Cubes or Small Bags
**Keeps:** 1 month

Portion: banana chunks + spinach + frozen berries or mango. Freeze in trays or small bags. Add Greek yogurt, milk, and protein powder when blending. 90-second breakfast.

## Building the 2–4 Week Buffer

The buffer doesn't exist after one prep session. Build it over 3–4 Sundays by making extra of whatever you're cooking that week.

**Target inventory (2-week buffer):**

| Item | Portions |
|------|---------|
| Dal (any variety) | 10–12 cups |
| Rajma or chole | 6–8 cups |
| Tomato-onion masala base | 20–24 × 2 oz cubes |
| Palak base | 6 cups |
| Soup (lentil/vegetable) | 10–12 cups |
| Cooked grains (rice/quinoa) | 8–10 cups |
| Roti | 15–20 pieces |
| Smoothie packs | 8–10 |

**How to maintain it:** When you pull something out, note it. Every 2–3 weeks do a freezer audit — see what's running low and prioritize it in the next prep session. You're not trying to eat the entire buffer down — you're maintaining it at a steady level.

## Reheating Guide

| Method | Best For | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Microwave | Dal, curry, soup, rice | Cover loosely, 2–3 min for 1-cup portions, stir halfway |
| Stovetop | Curries, masala base | Add splash of water, medium heat, stir often |
| Tawa (dry pan) | Roti, paratha | From frozen, 1–2 min per side |
| Blender | Smoothie packs | Blend from frozen — no thaw needed |

**Thawing overnight:** Move portions from freezer to fridge the night before if you prefer stovetop reheating. Not required — most things reheat fine from frozen.

## Key Considerations

**Cool food completely before filling trays.** Hot food in silicone trays = condensation = ice crystals = freezer burn and clumping. Let it reach room temperature, then refrigerate if needed before portioning.

**Don't fill to the brim.** Liquid expands when frozen. Fill 90% to avoid overflow and stuck lids.

**2 oz cubes are underrated.** Most people only use them for baby food, but for flavor bases, chutneys, coconut milk, and tamarind paste they're the most useful size in the system. Freeze a batch of fresh herb paste (cilantro + garlic + green chilli) in 2 oz cubes — instant flavor bomb.

**Rotation discipline.** New bags go in the back, older bags come to the front. Eat the oldest first.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Freezing food too hot** — ice crystals form inside, texture degrades, containers fog up and stick together.
2. **No labels** — dal, rajma, and palak base all look brown-green after a month. You will not remember which is which.
3. **Trying to freeze everything at once** — you'll fill the freezer in one session but have nothing to pull from for 4 months because it all expires at the same time. Stagger the batches.
4. **Not returning trays to circulation** — trays parked in the freezer full of frozen food = you can never make more. Pop and bag within 24 hours of freezing.
5. **Freezing items that degrade badly** — potatoes in curry, raw yogurt, salad components. Refer to the Do Not Freeze list.
