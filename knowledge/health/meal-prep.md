# Meal Prep
*The weekly system for having protein-ready food in the fridge so nutrition doesn't collapse mid-week.*

---

## What Is It
Meal prep is the practice of batching food preparation on one or two days so the rest of the week requires minimal decisions. It's not about cooking every meal in advance — it's about eliminating the "what do I eat right now" problem when you're tired, hungry, and the default is whatever's easiest.

The constraint it solves: on a vegetarian diet with a protein target, you cannot improvise. If there's nothing ready, you default to carbs.

## Why It Matters
Most nutrition failures aren't knowledge failures — they're friction failures. You know what to eat. You don't have it ready. Meal prep removes that gap.

One focused session on Sunday = five days of low-friction eating. The ROI on two hours of prep is dramatically higher than trying to cook every weeknight.

## The Sunday System

### What to Batch (Priority Order)

**1. A legume base** — pick one per week, rotate
- Dal (toor, moong, masoor) — lasts 4–5 days, serves as lunch or dinner protein base
- Rajma (kidney beans) — 2–3 servings ready to go
- Chole (chickpeas) — same
- Cook a large pot. Season well. Reheat with a quick tadka if needed.

**2. A high-protein main** — paneer, tofu, or soy chunks
- Paneer: cube and marinate in spices. Pan-fry or bake. Keeps 4–5 days.
- Tofu: press, cube, season, pan-fry or air-fry until crisp. Keeps 4 days.
- Soy chunks: rehydrate, squeeze dry, cook in a dry masala. Keeps 5 days.

**3. Eggs — boil a batch**
- 6–8 boiled eggs. Peeled and stored in the fridge. Grab-and-go protein for any meal.
- Takes 12 minutes. No excuse not to.

**4. Overnight oats or yogurt bowls**
- 2–3 portions of overnight oats in mason jars: oats + Greek yogurt + chia seeds + milk + whey protein. Grab from fridge, eat cold or warm.
- 5 minutes to assemble Sunday night = 3 mornings handled.

**5. Roasted snacks**
- Roasted chana, makhana, or mixed nuts portioned into small containers.
- Eliminates mid-afternoon snack decisions.

**6. Grains (optional)**
- Brown rice or quinoa: cook a large batch. Refrigerates well for 4–5 days.
- Roti made fresh daily is better — but if you batch-cook, freeze with parchment between each.

## Time Estimate
| Task | Time |
|------|------|
| Dal or legume base | 45 min (mostly hands-off) |
| Paneer/tofu/soy prep | 20 min |
| Boiled eggs | 12 min |
| Overnight oats (3 jars) | 10 min |
| Roasted snacks | 20 min (mostly oven) |
| **Total active time** | ~60–75 min |

Most of this runs simultaneously. Start the dal, prep the paneer while it simmers, boil eggs, assemble oats while the paneer cools.

## Weekly Grocery List

Organized by section. This is a full-coverage list — buy what you need for the week, not everything at once.

### Produce
- Spinach or mixed greens (salads, cooking base)
- Onions (cooking staple — always)
- Tomatoes (cooking base)
- Garlic + ginger (or paste)
- Green chillies
- Bell peppers (stir-fry, omelettes)
- Lemons / limes
- Bananas (smoothies, snacks)
- Whatever vegetables you'll use: zucchini, broccoli, cauliflower, sweet potato

### Protein Sources
- Paneer — 400–500g (full week if it's your main protein)
- Tofu — 1–2 blocks (extra firm)
- Greek yogurt — 500g–1kg tub
- Eggs — 1 dozen
- Cottage cheese — 250g
- Whey protein — already stocked; replenish when low
- Moong/toor/masoor dal — 500g bag (lasts multiple weeks)
- Chickpeas — 1 can or dry bag
- Rajma — 1 can or dry bag
- Soy chunks — 200g bag (lasts weeks)

### Dairy
- Milk — 1–2 liters
- Paneer (listed above)
- Greek yogurt (listed above)
- Ghee — small jar; replenish monthly

### Grains & Staples
- Whole wheat atta (flour) for roti — already stocked; replenish as needed
- Brown rice or basmati — 1 bag
- Oats (rolled) — 500g bag
- Quinoa — optional, 250g

### Nuts, Seeds & Snacks
- Almonds — 200g
- Peanuts or peanut butter — 250g jar
- Chia seeds — 100g bag (lasts weeks)
- Hemp seeds — 100g bag
- Roasted chana — 200g bag
- Makhana — 100g bag

### Pantry (restock as needed)
- Olive oil / coconut oil
- Besan (chickpea flour) — for cheela
- Canned tomatoes or tomato paste
- Spices: cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, chili powder, amchur
- Vegetable broth or stock cubes

### Supplements
- Whey protein
- B12 supplement
- Vitamin D3 supplement

## Sample Assembled Week

**Monday–Friday pattern (after Sunday prep):**

| Meal | What's ready |
|------|-------------|
| Breakfast | Overnight oats jar from fridge OR eggs + toast |
| Lunch | Dal + paneer/tofu + roti or rice |
| Snack | Boiled egg + roasted chana + yogurt |
| Dinner | Legume curry (rajma/chole/dal) + grain |
| Post-workout | Whey shake |

You're not eating the same thing every day — you're mixing the prepped components into different combinations. Dal one day as lunch, same dal in a khichdi the next.

## Key Considerations

**Rotate proteins week to week.** If you do paneer every week, you'll burn out. Rotate: paneer week → tofu week → soy chunks week → eggs-heavy week. Same system, different inputs.

**Label everything.** Date the containers. "Is this dal from this Sunday or last Sunday?" kills the system slowly.

**Don't prep food you don't actually want to eat.** If you hate the taste of soy chunks, don't make them. The most nutritious food is the one you'll actually eat.

**Keep the system simple.** Two protein sources + one legume base + boiled eggs + overnight oats = done. Don't try to batch five different dishes — you'll spend three hours cooking and burn out on prep itself.

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Over-prepping variety** — cooking six different things to avoid boredom, then spending 3 hours in the kitchen. Two or three items done well is enough.
2. **Not labeling/dating** — you'll throw out food you're not sure about, which defeats the purpose.
3. **Prepping without a plan** — know which meals you're covering before you start. Prep without a target wastes ingredients.
4. **Skipping the snack prep** — portioned snacks are what prevents the 3pm vending machine decision. Don't skip them.
5. **Not restocking mid-week** — boiled eggs and overnight oats are fast enough to make mid-week when they run out. Don't wait for Sunday.
