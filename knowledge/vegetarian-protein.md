# Vegetarian Protein — Quick Reference

## Why This File Exists
Hitting 0.8–1g protein per lb bodyweight on a vegetarian diet is my biggest nutrition
challenge. This is my go-to reference so I never have to think "what has protein?" —
I just look here and build meals.

## My Target
Assuming ~160 lbs: **128–160g protein per day**
Split across 4 eating windows: ~35-40g per meal/snack

## Protein Source Cheat Sheet

### Tier 1 — Heavy Hitters (15g+ per serving)
| Food | Protein | Serving | Notes |
|------|---------|---------|-------|
| Soy chunks (dry) | ~50g/100g | 50g dry = ~25g | Rehydrate, use in curry or stir-fry |
| Paneer | ~18g/100g | 150g = ~27g | Tikka, bhurji, palak paneer |
| Greek yogurt | ~10g/100g | 200g = ~20g | Breakfast bowls, smoothies, raita |
| Lentils/dal (cooked) | ~9g/100g | 1 cup = ~18g | Dal, khichdi, soups |
| Chickpeas (cooked) | ~8g/100g | 1 cup = ~15g | Chole, hummus, roasted snack |
| Rajma (cooked) | ~8g/100g | 1 cup = ~15g | Rajma chawal — comfort + protein |
| Tofu | ~15g/100g | 150g = ~22g | Stir-fry, bhurji style, grilled |
| Eggs | ~6g each | 3 eggs = ~18g | Omelette, boiled, bhurji |
| Whey protein | ~24g/scoop | 1 scoop = ~24g | Post-workout shake, quick fix |

### Tier 2 — Solid Supporting Sources (5-15g per serving)

## Sample Day — Hitting 140g+ Protein

**Breakfast (~35g)**
- 200g Greek yogurt (20g) + 1 scoop whey mixed in (24g) + chia seeds (2g)
- OR: 3-egg omelette (18g) + 2 toast with peanut butter (8g) + glass of milk (10g)

**Lunch (~35g)**
- 1 cup dal (18g) + 150g paneer in sabzi (27g) + roti
- OR: Chickpea curry (15g) + quinoa (8g) + raita with Greek yogurt (10g)

**Snack (~20g)**
- Roasted chana (10g) + handful of almonds (6g) + glass of milk (5g)
- OR: Peanut butter banana smoothie with milk (15-20g)

**Dinner (~35g)**
- Rajma (15g) + rice + tofu stir-fry (22g)
- OR: Soy chunk curry (25g) + dal (10g) + roti

**Post-workout (if needed) (~24g)**
- Whey shake with milk

## Meal Prep Staples (Sunday Prep)
These are the things to batch cook so the week doesn't fall apart:
1. **Big pot of dal** — lasts 3-4 days, base for multiple meals
2. **Cooked chickpeas or rajma** — store in fridge, add to anything
3. **Paneer cubes** — marinate and keep ready for quick tikka or bhurji
4. **Boiled eggs** — grab and go protein
5. **Overnight oats with protein** — Greek yogurt + oats + chia + whey = grab from fridge
6. **Roasted chana / makhana** — portion into snack bags

## Key Combos for Complete Amino Acids
Plant proteins are often "incomplete" — combine these for full profiles:
- **Dal + rice** — the classic Indian complete protein combo
- **Roti + hummus** — wheat + chickpea covers all amino acids
- **Rice + rajma** — legume + grain
- **Tofu + quinoa** — both close to complete on their own

You don't need to combine in the same meal — just eat variety across the day.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Relying only on dal** — it's good but 1 cup is only ~18g. You need way more.
2. **Forgetting dairy** — paneer, Greek yogurt, milk, and cottage cheese are protein gold for vegetarians.
3. **Not prepping** — if there's no ready-to-eat protein in the fridge, you'll default to carbs.
4. **Counting protein from rice/roti** — these have some protein but it's minimal. Don't count on them.
5. **Skipping whey** — no shame in using a supplement to close the gap, especially on busy days.
