# The Book of Tenets — Plan

## What This Is

A personal wisdom text built on the 7 Core Tenets. Not a religion with theology or a self-help book with steps. A living document — plain language, drawn from the deepest agreement across every philosophical tradition, written for practical use.

The goal: something you can read any morning and actually remember by evening.

---

## The 7 Tenets (working titles)

1. **The mind makes the world** — suffering is constructed, not delivered
2. **Holding tight makes it worse** — attachment is the mechanism of suffering
3. **You are not alone in here** — the self is less separate than it feels
4. **Now is the only place** — the present moment is the only real address
5. **You become what you do** — virtue is habit, not intention
6. **Meaning is made, not found** — engagement creates purpose; waiting does not
7. **The small self gets in the way** — ego is the obstacle between you and clarity

---

## Structure of Each Tenet Section

Every tenet follows the same format:

```
# Tenet [N]: [Short Title]

## The Statement
One sentence. Clear, memorable, no jargon.

## Quotes
2–3 quotes — one Western, one Eastern, one modern where possible.
Short attribution. No long explanations here.

## What This Actually Means
Plain prose. 2–3 paragraphs. No philosophy-speak.
What is this saying? Why does it matter?

## What It Looks Like in Real Life
2–4 concrete, specific examples.
Not abstract. Situations a person actually faces.

## What It Is Not
1–2 common misreadings. Clarify the edge.

## Remember
3–5 bullet points. The takeaways you can hold in your head.
Short enough to memorize.
```

---

## Tone

- Direct. No padding, no hedging, no corporate inspiration-speak.
- Serious but not solemn. Wise without being preachy.
- Slightly elevated — this is a text meant to be read slowly, not skimmed.
- No cheerleading. No "you've got this." Just the truth, plainly stated.
- Stoic in backbone, Zen in texture.
