# Atlantic County / Galloway Township Guide
*Galloway-specific services, offices, taxes, courts, and the local landscape — what you actually need to know as a resident*

---

## What Is It

This file covers Atlantic County and Galloway Township as a resident's operating manual: where to go, who to call, what to file, and how local government actually works. It also covers the broader Atlantic County development landscape and what living in Galloway means geographically and institutionally.

Atlantic County is a mid-sized South Jersey county of ~270,000 people. Atlantic City is the county seat and most visible landmark, but most of the county's residential life happens in surrounding townships — Galloway, Egg Harbor, Hamilton, Hammonton. Galloway Township is the largest municipality by land area in New Jersey (170+ square miles) and one of the largest by population in the county (~37,000 people).

---

## Why It Matters

You live here. Knowing your township's services means not overpaying taxes you're entitled to reduce, not missing deadlines, not driving to the wrong office, and knowing who represents you when something needs to change. Local government has direct power over your property taxes, your land use, your road, your water — and most people never engage it until there's a crisis.

## Core Concepts

### Galloway Township Government

**Structure**: Galloway operates under a Township Committee form of government — five elected committee members, one of whom serves as Mayor on a rotating or elected basis. They meet publicly twice a month (first and third Wednesday). Meetings are posted on the township website and often streamed.

**Key offices:**
- **Township Clerk**: Licenses, meeting agendas/minutes, OPRA requests (public records). This is your first call for anything administrative.
- **Tax Collector**: Property tax payments, due dates, liens. Tax bills are quarterly — February, May, August, November.
- **Tax Assessor**: Property valuations, exemption applications, assessment appeals. Different from the collector — assessor sets the value, collector collects based on it.
- **Construction/Zoning**: Building permits, variance applications, zoning questions. If you're adding a structure, renovating significantly, or doing anything that changes land use, start here.
- **Public Works**: Road issues, potholes, storm drains, trash/recycling schedules.
- **Municipal Court**: Traffic violations, local ordinance violations, some small claims. Located at Township Hall.

**Township Hall address**: 300 E. Jimmie Leeds Rd, Galloway, NJ 08205. Most offices are here.

**Website**: gallowaytwp-nj.gov

### Atlantic County Government

The county layer sits above the township and handles functions too large for individual municipalities:

- **Atlantic County Executive + Freeholder Board (Board of County Commissioners)**: County-level policy, budget, county roads. Seven commissioners elected by district.
- **County Clerk**: Deeds, mortgages, passport applications, voter registration, marriage licenses.
- **Surrogate's Court**: Wills, probate, guardianship.
- **Prosecutor's Office**: Criminal prosecution for the county.
- **Sheriff**: County jail, courthouse security, civil process (eviction notices, etc.).
- **Office of Emergency Management**: Disaster prep, county alerts.
- **Atlantic County Library System**: Main branch in May's Landing (county seat), branch in Galloway (Jimmie Leeds Rd). Free library card, digital resources, notary services.

**County seat**: Mays Landing (Hamilton Township) — not Atlantic City, which is a common misconception.

### Tax Assessor — The Most Important Office to Know

Atlantic County uses a standard NJ assessment process. Your property tax = assessed value × tax rate. The rate is set annually; the assessment is the assessor's estimate of your property's value.

**Key actions:**
- **Homestead Benefit / ANCHOR**: Apply through the state (see [nj-benefits-and-programs.md](nj-benefits-and-programs.md)) — but the assessed value is the local figure.
- **Assessment appeal**: If you believe your home is over-assessed (assessed above fair market value), you can appeal to the Atlantic County Board of Taxation. Deadline is typically April 1 of the tax year. You need comparable sales data (comps) to make the case. This is worth doing if your assessed value is clearly above what the house would sell for.
- **Exemptions**: Veterans, disabled veterans, senior citizens — these reduce assessed value or provide a credit. Apply through the Assessor's office.

### Municipal Court

Galloway Municipal Court handles:
- Local ordinance violations (noise, code enforcement)
- Motor vehicle offenses that occurred in Galloway
- Disorderly persons offenses (NJ's equivalent of misdemeanors) within jurisdiction
- Parking tickets

If you get a ticket in Galloway, you can often pay online, request an adjournment, or request a hearing. For anything that could affect your driving record or insurance, showing up and contesting is almost always worth it — judges have discretion and prosecutors will often offer downgraded charges (especially for speeding tickets: "unsafe driving" instead of a speeding violation avoids insurance points).

### Stockton University

Richard Stockton College (now Stockton University) is located in Galloway Township, near the town center. It has ~10,000 students and is the dominant institution in the area. It affects Galloway in a few ways:
- Employment: One of the larger non-service employers in the area
- Housing: Student rental demand affects housing stock around the campus area
- Events/Culture: Public events, concerts, lectures — open to community, often free
- The Stockton Coastal Research Center operates out of Galloway and does local environmental work

### Development Landscape

Galloway is partly suburban, partly rural, and has significant Pinelands (Pine Barrens) protection over portions of its land area. This matters for development:

**Pinelands Commission**: A regional authority (not just a state agency) that controls land use in the Pinelands area. Much of Galloway's western land falls under Pinelands regulations, which restrict impervious surface, septic density, and development types. If you own or are considering buying land in a Pinelands area, development is heavily constrained — even adding a structure can require Pinelands approval on top of local permits.

**Growth areas**: The US-9 corridor (Jimmie Leeds Rd, Black Horse Pike) is where most commercial and residential development happens. The Smithville area (a historic village turned tourist/shopping area) is in Galloway.

**Sewerage**: Parts of Galloway are on municipal sewer; parts rely on septic systems. This affects what you can build and what your maintenance responsibilities are. Check with the township if you're unsure which applies to your property.

### Utilities and Services

- **Electric/Gas**: Atlantic City Electric (ACE) for most of Galloway; South Jersey Gas for natural gas.
- **Water**: Varies by area — some areas are on Atlantic County municipal utilities, others have well water. New Jersey American Water serves some areas.
- **Trash/Recycling**: Township-run; schedule varies by neighborhood. Single-stream recycling. Bulk pickup is scheduled (not on-demand) — watch the township calendar.
- **Emergency services**: Galloway has a township police department. Fire is volunteer-based with multiple companies across the large township footprint.

### Voter Registration and Civic Participation

- Register at the Atlantic County Clerk's office or online (nj.gov/state/elections).
- Galloway is split across multiple legislative districts — check your specific address for your state assembly/senate representative.
- County Commissioners are elected by district; know yours.
- Township Committee elections are in November of odd years (NJ local election cycle).
- Planning Board and Zoning Board meetings are public and are where real decisions about the township's physical development get made. These are often more impactful to your daily life than the Committee meetings.

## Key Considerations

**OPRA requests**: Under NJ's Open Public Records Act, you can request almost any government document — meeting minutes, contracts, permits, inspection reports. File with the Township Clerk. Response required within 7 business days. This is genuinely useful if you're trying to understand something about your neighborhood, a neighbor's project, or a local issue.

**Tax appeal timing**: You have until April 1 to appeal your assessment for that year. If you miss it, you wait another year. Mark this date.

**Pinelands constraints are real**: If you're buying land or thinking about major additions, check whether the parcel is in the Pinelands area *before* making plans. The Pinelands Commission website has a mapping tool.

**Volunteer fire departments**: Because fire service is volunteer-based across a large area, response times in remote parts of the township can be longer than in denser areas. Factor into home safety planning (smoke detectors, fire extinguishers, egress).

**Smithville**: If you haven't been, it's worth knowing — it's a tourist-oriented historic village within Galloway with shops and restaurants. Also the location of the Smithville Inn and surrounding development. Not relevant to government, but useful local geography.

## Getting Started

If you haven't done these, do them now:

1. **Find your property record**: Go to gallowaytwp-nj.gov or the Atlantic County tax records portal. Look up your address. Know your assessed value, lot and block number, and tax rate. This is your baseline.
2. **Check for exemptions you qualify for**: Veteran? Disabled? Senior over 65? Apply at the Assessor's office. These are often unclaimed.
3. **Confirm your trash/recycling schedule**: Sounds trivial — but knowing the schedule avoids missed pickups on major items.
4. **Register to vote (if not)**: atcnj.us (Atlantic County Clerk) — or nj.gov/state/elections.
5. **Save the township number**: (609) 652-3700 — general Galloway Township line.
6. **Check your water/sewer**: Know whether you're on municipal water or well, and whether you have septic or sewer. Affects what you're responsible for.

## Common Mistakes

**Confusing the Assessor and Tax Collector**: Two different offices with two different functions. The Assessor sets your value; the Collector takes your payment. Appeals go to the Assessor/County Board of Taxation, not the Collector.

**Assuming Atlantic City is the county seat**: It's Mays Landing. Some county offices are in Atlantic City (like the courthouse), but the county administrative offices are in Mays Landing.

**Ignoring Pinelands rules before starting a project**: Getting a local permit doesn't mean you're Pinelands-compliant. They're separate approvals.

**Missing the tax appeal deadline**: April 1 is firm. If your assessed value is too high, act before then.

**Not knowing your legislative district**: NJ has Assembly, Senate, and Congressional representatives by district. Your Galloway address maps to specific people. Worth knowing when something affects you.
