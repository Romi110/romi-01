# One Piece — Ending Theory and Plot Hole Resolution
*The most complete reasoning for how One Piece ends — what the One Piece is, who Imu is, what the Void Century was, and how every major thread closes*

---

## What Is It

This file synthesizes Oda's confirmed reveals, long-running narrative foreshadowing, structural parallels, and the most internally consistent fan reasoning to project how One Piece ends. This is not speculation for its own sake — it's the most probable ending given what Oda has already told us, reading the story as a tightly constructed text with a single author who planned the ending before chapter 1.

Oda has confirmed he knows the ending. He wrote it early. The question is reading the structure back from what's been revealed.

## The Void Century: What Actually Happened

**The reconstruction:**

~900 years ago, an advanced civilization existed — the **Ancient Kingdom**. It was the most technologically advanced society in the world. It created the Poneglyphs (indestructible so its history could survive), built the Ancient Weapons, and had a philosophy of freedom — its patron deity or symbol was the Sun God Nika, a figure representing liberation, laughter, and the freedom to live as you wish.

Its king or champion was **Joy Boy** — a man with the Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Nika (the same fruit Luffy has now). Joy Boy was attempting to fulfill a promise: to bring the Fishman people to the surface world using the Noah (a ship big enough to transport an entire population), powered by Poseidon's ability to command Sea Kings.

~800 years ago, the **20 Kings** — the rulers of 20 nations — formed an alliance and made war on the Ancient Kingdom. They won. The Ancient Kingdom was destroyed. The 20 Kings created the **World Government** from their alliance, abandoned their home countries (which is why their empty thrones exist in each nation), and moved to Mary Geoise at the top of the Red Line.

**What was erased:** The Ancient Kingdom's name, history, technology, and philosophy. The World Government declared the 100 years of that war the "Void Century" and prohibited its study on pain of death. The Ohara massacre (20 years before the story begins) was the most recent enforcement of that prohibition.

**Why they won:** The 20 Kings had the Five Elders or their predecessors, who are ancient mythical Zoan users and effectively immortal — possibly the original beneficiaries of the Ope Ope no Mi's immortality operation. They have ruled for 800 years through proxy governance (letting the World Government kings believe they hold power), suppression of history, and the threat of Buster Calls.

**Joy Boy's failure:** He promised Fishman Island he would use Noah to bring them to the surface. At the critical moment — presumably the fall of the Ancient Kingdom — he couldn't fulfill it. He left a message at Laughtale acknowledging the failure and expressing faith that someone would come centuries later to complete what he started. That person is Luffy, the next person to inherit Nika's fruit.

## What the One Piece Is

**The most supported conclusion:** The One Piece is Joy Boy's treasure chest — containing a **letter** (Joy Boy's apology/message to Poseidon and Fishman Island, already known from the Fishman Island Poneglyph) and **the proof of Joy Boy's existence**: his straw hat.

The Giant Straw Hat held in Mary Geoise — kept frozen in a vault in a pose of reverence — is Joy Boy's original hat. The lineage of straw hats (Joy Boy → Roger → Shanks → Luffy) is the lineage of people who carry Joy Boy's will. The treasure at Laughtale is the confirmation that this will exists and the world's true history.

But the One Piece is not merely an object — its **value is what it enables.** Roger reached Laughtale, read Joy Boy's letter, understood the world's true history, and laughed. He said he and his crew had "the wrong era" — they arrived too early. The age of Joy Boy's return required a specific kind of person (someone with the Nika fruit) and a specific moment (when the world was ready to change). Roger died at the Marines' hands, launching the Pirate Age to accelerate the search.

The announcement that One Piece exists at Roger's execution did exactly what Roger intended: it set the world in motion so that by the time someone reaches Laughtale, the conditions for the final war are in place.

**The One Piece cannot be the "boring" answer (just gold).** Oda has built 25 years of narrative weight around it. The functional answer is: the truth of the world + the inherited will of Joy Boy + the straw hat that symbolizes freedom. When Luffy reaches Laughtale, he will understand why Roger laughed — because the treasure is the confirmation that the world can be changed, not the means to change it.

## Who Imu Is

**The most coherent theory:** Imu is one of the original 20 Kings — or the one king who was elevated above the others in exchange for something. They received immortality through the Ope Ope no Mi's ultimate technique, performed 800 years ago by an Ope Ope no Mi user who gave their life for it.

This is why:
- Imu has existed (presumably) since the World Government's founding
- The Gorosei answer to Imu, not the other way around — the five Elders are near-immortal themselves but still subordinate
- The Empty Throne has always had one occupant — hidden
- Imu's goal is the preservation of the current world order — not conquest of new territory, but the suppression of change itself

**Imu's power:** Imu possesses the **Ito Ito no Mi, Model: Perenna** or more likely a **Paramecia of erasure/negation** — Imu literally erased the kingdom of Lulusia from the map in a single panel by pointing at a globe. This is a weapon-scale power, potentially an ancient weapon itself (Uranus — the third ancient weapon, never named in-universe until Egghead hints).

**Uranus = Imu.** The three ancient weapons are:
- Pluton: the warship, blueprints held by Franky (destroyed after Wano)
- Poseidon: Shirahoshi, the mermaid princess who can command Sea Kings
- Uranus: the weapon in the hands of the World Government — likely Imu's personal weapon or Imu themselves

This closes the ancient weapons plot hole: why the World Government is so powerful militarily even without its own ancient weapon visible. They have Uranus. It's been Imu all along.

## The Will of D

**The D clan are the descendants (biological or ideological) of the Ancient Kingdom's people** — specifically those who refused to surrender when the 20 Kings won and who escaped or were dispersed across the world.

The D has two interpretations that are both probably true:
1. **Name:** The initial D comes from the Ancient Kingdom's name — which the World Government erased. D is the surviving letter or abbreviation of a name that can no longer be spoken. The Gorosei call D "the natural enemy of the gods" (Celestial Dragons are the descendants of the 20 Kings — self-declared gods).
2. **Will:** The D is not strictly genetic — it's an inherited conviction. People bearing D carry, consciously or not, the will of the Ancient Kingdom: the belief that freedom matters more than order, that people should live as they wish, that the current world is wrong.

This is why D people tend toward either enormous ambition (Roger, Luffy, Blackbeard) or protection of others (Garp, Ace, Saul) — both expressions of the same core conviction, applied differently.

**Nefertari Vivi is D.** The Nefertari family was one of the 20 Kings — but they refused to move to Mary Geoise. They stayed in Alabasta to rule their people. Cobra (Vivi's father) was killed at the Levely for asking Im about his ancestor's decision. The Nefertari D is the surviving proof that even within the 20 Kings, there were dissenters — people who chose their people over power.

## Blackbeard's Two Devil Fruits

**The problem:** A person who eats two Devil Fruits dies. Blackbeard (Marshall D. Teach) ate Whitebeard's Tremor-Tremor Fruit in addition to his own Darkness-Darkness Fruit at Marineford — on screen — and did not die.

**The solution:** Blackbeard's body is abnormal. Marco observed this on Whitebeard's ship: "What's inside that body?" The most consistent theory is that Blackbeard has multiple souls or multiple bodies — a parasitic twin or a chimera-like condition. He sleeps differently, eats differently, and has always been described as uniquely monstrous in physicality.

The Darkness-Darkness Fruit allows Blackbeard to nullify Devil Fruit powers by touching people. Its awakening is most likely the ability to permanently absorb and contain other Devil Fruits — effectively making the "death" that normally kills someone with two fruits into a process his body can survive because it holds multiple "slots."

This also explains why Blackbeard has been collecting Devil Fruits throughout the New World using his crew: he is building an arsenal of fruit powers he can eventually wield or distribute. His endgame is to become a one-man army of Devil Fruit powers.

## The Final War

**Structure:** The final war is Luffy's alliance (Straw Hats + Revolutionary Army + various allied nations and former enemies) versus the World Government (Marines + Gorosei + Imu + whatever Blackbeard does).

**The sequence:**
1. Luffy reaches Laughtale with at least one Road Poneglyph. Roger's log / Joy Boy's treasure is found.
2. The truth of the Void Century is broadcast to the world (Vegapunk's partial broadcast accelerated this — the full truth arrives at Laughtale).
3. The world splinters: nations that have been under World Government control must choose sides. The Reverie's fractures (Vivi's disappearance, Sabo's witness of something catastrophic, the Revolutionaries' public attack) have already primed this.
4. Imu activates Uranus to suppress the rebellion — erasing nations or individuals.
5. The ancient weapons converge: Luffy (as successor to Joy Boy, effectively) commands or aligns with Poseidon (Shirahoshi / Sea Kings) and Pluton (the warship, activated from Wano's harbor).
6. The final battle is Luffy vs. Imu — freedom vs. the 800-year order.

**Shanks:** Shanks knows everything Roger knew. He has been positioning himself to manage the endgame, not to win it — he wants Luffy to be the one who arrives at the right moment. His interference with Whitebeard before Marineford (stopping Whitebeard from going to save Ace) was an attempt to prevent the war from happening at the wrong time. His killing of Kid in the Elbaf arc demonstrates that he is not passively waiting — he is clearing obstacles and managing who reaches Laughtale.

**Shanks will not be a villain.** He will be the person who, at the crucial moment, stands aside — or sacrifices himself — to ensure Luffy's path to Laughtale is unobstructed. The Straw Hat lineage ends with Luffy. Shanks' role is the penultimate guardian, not the final boss.

**Blackbeard** is the true penultimate antagonist — the dark mirror of Luffy. Both are D, both are aiming for One Piece and King of the Pirates. The difference: Luffy wants freedom for everyone; Blackbeard wants power for himself. Their final fight is the series' thematic resolution. Law will likely sacrifice himself to remove Blackbeard's fruit hoard (using the Ope Ope no Mi's "Room" to extract or disable the stored fruits), replaying Corazon's sacrifice.

## The Dawn of the World

"The Dawn of the World" is what Joy Boy promised — what Roger waited for — what Oden died for — what Whitebeard declared would come. It is not a metaphor. It is a specific change:

**The world floods.** Vegapunk confirmed this: the rising sea level is real and ongoing. The Ancient Weapons, Joy Boy's failure, and the current political order are all connected to the world's future environmental state. Poseidon's role — commanding Sea Kings — and Noah's role (a ship large enough to carry an entire people) are not incidental. They are infrastructure for **relocating the world's population** when the seas rise.

Joy Boy's original promise was to bring the Fishman people to the surface using Noah and Poseidon. His failure was not just a personal failure — it was a failure to complete the migration infrastructure before the Ancient Kingdom fell. Eight hundred years later, the problem is still there, and the sea is still rising.

"The Dawn" is:
1. The destruction of the Empty Throne and Imu's 800-year suppression of history
2. The revelation of the truth — the world's real history, broadcast to everyone
3. The beginning of a new era without the World Government's false order
4. Poseidon/Luffy enabling the migration of Fishman Island residents to the surface — Joy Boy's original promise finally kept
5. A world where people can live freely, including on the open sea without political subjugation

## Remaining Plot Holes Closed

**Why did Shanks eat Luffy's fruit?** Shanks gave up his arm to save Luffy. He ate the Hito Hito no Mi, Model: Nika at some point — or he acquired it from Roger's voyage and saved it deliberately. Shanks chose Luffy to be the fruit's next user, feeding him the fruit intentionally. This is why Shanks is not threatened by the fruit being "wasted" (eaten accidentally) — he chose Luffy for it.

**Why does Blackbeard sleep differently?** His multi-body physiology. His massive size, unusual eating habits, and extreme sleepiness all point to a body maintaining multiple lives simultaneously.

**What happened to Sabo at the Levely?** Sabo witnessed Imu on the Empty Throne — and saw Im about to use Uranus on a populated location. The "incident" that the newspapers reported about the Levely was the erasure of Lulusia Kingdom, which Sabo was in. He survived (confirmed later) but the world was told he died, creating the Revolutionary Army's martyrdom narrative.

**What is the Seraphim's role?** The Seraphim (child clones of former Warlords: Mihawk, Hancock, Jinbei, Crocodile, Kuma) are Vegapunk's attempt to create powerful weapons outside World Government control. York (the traitor satellite) handed them to the Gorosei. They become either tools of the World Government in the final war or — if someone takes control of their control chips — allies. Likely the latter, following One Piece's pattern of "enemy turned ally through emotional resonance."

**Why didn't Robin just read Poneglyphs earlier?** She spent 20 years running — she was never in a position to do so safely. The Straw Hats gave her the first real protection she had since Ohara. Even so, the Road Poneglyphs required reaching all four locations, each guarded by a Yonko. The crew spent the entire post-timeskip dismantling each Yonko's territory to access the Poneglyphs. It's not a plot hole — it's the plot.

**What is Monkey D. Dragon's power?** Dragon is the Revolutionary Army's leader and the most wanted man in the world. He has never used a named power on screen. The most consistent theory: Dragon has a Wind or Storm Logia fruit — his appearances are always accompanied by wind and storms (Loguetown, Goa Kingdom). It would make him one of the most powerful characters in the series and explain how the Revolutionary Army has survived the World Government's pressure.

**What happened to Vegapunk's satellites?** Pythagoras was destroyed at Egghead. York betrayed the others and was taken by the Gorosei. The surviving satellites (Shaka, Lilith, Edison, Atlas) escape with the Straw Hats and become crew-adjacent — likely the scientific infrastructure for the new world after the final war.

## What "King of the Pirates" Means at the End

Roger became King of the Pirates by reaching Laughtale. He couldn't fulfill Joy Boy's promise — wrong era. He died laughing, knowing someone would come.

When Luffy reaches Laughtale and becomes King of the Pirates, the title will mean something different than it has throughout the series. Roger's era asked: who is strong enough to reach Laughtale? Luffy's era asks: who is willing to change the world once they get there?

Luffy will not sit on a throne. He will not rule. He will do exactly what he said he would at the beginning: become free. The "freedom" One Piece promises is not personal — it's the freedom of the entire world from 800 years of suppressed history and political control. Luffy's King of the Pirates is not a title of power. It is the title of the person who made the world's Dawn finally arrive.

The final image — widely theorized and consistent with Oda's own early sketches — is Luffy alone on a small boat, laughing. Not ruling. Not settling. Sailing freely. His crew disbands to pursue their own dreams (Zoro vs. Mihawk settled; Nami's complete world map drawn; Sanji's All Blue found; Robin learning the full history; etc.). And somewhere in the world, the truth is known, and the people are free to write their own history.
