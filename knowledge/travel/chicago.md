# Chicago
*A weekend guide to the city — architecture, food, neighborhoods, and how to actually get around.*

---

## What Is It

Chicago is a walkable, transit-friendly Midwestern city on Lake Michigan. It's known for its architecture (genuinely world-class), deep-dish pizza (overrated as a daily eat — locals eat thin crust), jazz and blues history, and a strong neighborhood identity. For a weekend trip from New Jersey, it's a 2-hour direct flight and a very easy city to navigate.

Best seasons: **May–June** and **September–October**. July and August are hot, humid, and crowded. November–March is brutal cold.

---

## Getting There

**Flights**: O'Hare (ORD) or Midway (MDW). United/American hub at O'Hare; Southwest at Midway. From Newark (EWR), direct flights are ~2 hours. O'Hare is farther from the city; Midway is closer to the south side neighborhoods.

**Airport to city**: Blue Line train from O'Hare to downtown — $5, ~45 minutes, runs 24/7. Cheaper and often faster than an Uber during traffic. Orange Line from Midway. Uber/Lyft are an option but add $35–55 each way plus traffic.

**Getting around in the city**: The CTA L train covers the core well. A transit card (Ventra) is worth it for a weekend — $10 for unlimited rides. Uber works for gap coverage. Renting a car is unnecessary and parking is expensive.

---

## Where to Stay

**Best area**: River North, Streeterville, or West Loop. All are central — short transit or walk to most of the main attractions.

- **River North**: Most hotels, walkable to Magnificent Mile and Michigan Ave. Lively but touristy.
- **West Loop**: Better restaurant scene, slightly hipper, slightly less convenient to tourist sites.
- **Wicker Park/Bucktown**: Good for a more local feel; a short L ride from downtown.

Avoid the far south or far north unless you have a specific reason.

---

## Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

**The Loop** — Downtown financial district. Home to the Art Institute, Millennium Park (The Bean), and the Chicago Riverwalk. Most of the architecture tour stuff is here.

**River North** — Restaurants, bars, and galleries. Where most visitors end up eating.

**West Loop** — Chicago's best restaurant neighborhood. Randolph Street ("Restaurant Row") has the highest concentration of notable places.

**Wicker Park / Bucktown** — Indie shops, good brunch, younger crowd. Milwaukee Ave is the main drag. Take the Blue Line.

**Lincoln Park / Lakeview** — Residential, walkable, less touristy. Good for a morning walk by the lake.

**Pilsen** — Mexican neighborhood on the south side, good murals and food. Worth half a day if you want to get off the standard circuit.

**Hyde Park** — Home to UChicago and the Museum of Science and Industry. Far south, 30–40 min from downtown — worth it if museums are a priority.

## What to Do

### Architecture

Chicago has the best building stock in America. This is not optional.

- **Chicago Architecture Center River Cruise** — 90-minute boat tour on the Chicago River narrated by an architect. Book in advance. One of the best things you can do in any American city. ~$55.
- **Walking the Loop**: The El tracks, the Tribune Tower, the Rookery, the Wrigley Building. Just walking and looking is worthwhile.
- **Millennium Park** — Free. The Bean (Cloud Gate) is genuinely cool, not just a cliché. The surrounding park and Frank Gehry's Pritzker Pavilion are worth an hour.

### Museums

- **Art Institute of Chicago** — One of the best art museums in the country. Strong on Impressionism and American art. Plan 2–3 hours minimum. Book tickets in advance on weekends.
- **Museum of Science and Industry** — In Hyde Park, large and interactive. Better for families or if you're staying a full weekend.
- **Chicago History Museum** — Underrated. Good exhibit on the city's history and architecture. Usually uncrowded.

### The Lakefront

Lake Shore Drive and the lakefront trail run 18 miles along Lake Michigan. Rent a bike (Divvy bikes, available everywhere) and ride north from downtown to Lincoln Park. Free and worth a morning.

### Music and Nightlife

Chicago has real blues history. **Buddy Guy's Legends** (South Loop) is a working blues club, not a tourist trap — Buddy Guy still plays there occasionally. **Green Mill** in Uptown is a jazz club in a former speakeasy; $15 cover, cash only.

## Where to Eat

### Deep Dish

If you're going to have it, have it at one of the originals — not a tourist strip location.

- **Lou Malnati's** — Most consistent, widest locations. Butter crust, solid baseline.
- **Pequod's** (Lincoln Park) — Caramelized cheese crust on the edge, more flavor. Most locals prefer it. Waits can be long; go early or call ahead.
- **Giordano's** — Stuffed pizza (different from deep dish). Popular, but more tourist-facing.

Note: deep dish takes 45 minutes to cook. Plan accordingly. And eat it once — then eat like a local the rest of the trip (thin crust, tavern style).

### Thin Crust (Tavern Style)

Chicago's actual everyday pizza: thin, cracker-crust, cut in squares (party cut). Better for multiple meals.

- **Vito & Nick's** (south side) — The classic.
- **Piece** (Wicker Park) — New Haven-style, excellent.

### West Loop / Randolph Street

The highest concentration of good restaurants in the city. Book in advance.

- **Girl & the Goat** (Stephanie Izard) — Sharing plates, pork-heavy, creative. Book 2–3 weeks out.
- **Au Cheval** — Best burger in Chicago by most accounts. No reservations, expect a 1–2 hour wait. Go at 11am when they open or at 10pm.
- **Publican** — Pork, oysters, beer. Loud and communal. Excellent.

### Other

- **Avec** (West Loop) — Wine bar and small plates. Excellent, easier reservation.
- **Portillo's** — Chicago fast food institution. Italian beef sandwich or a Chicago-style hot dog (no ketchup — the locals will judge you). Cheap and correct.
- **Do-Rite Donuts** — If you want a donut, this is where.

### Chicago-Style Hot Dog

Correct build: yellow mustard, white onions, bright green relish, tomato wedges, sport peppers, celery salt, dill pickle spear — on a poppy seed bun. No ketchup. **Superdawg** (drive-in, northwest side) is iconic; **Portillo's** is more accessible.

## Sample 3-Day Itinerary

**Day 1 — Arrive + Downtown**
- Check in, drop bags
- Riverwalk + architecture walk in the Loop
- Dinner in River North or West Loop (book in advance)
- Blues at Buddy Guy's Legends

**Day 2 — Architecture + Art**
- Morning: Architecture River Cruise (book ahead)
- Lunch near the river
- Art Institute (2–3 hours)
- Millennium Park + Bean
- Dinner: Au Cheval (arrive at 10:45am for next-day opening, or wait it out)

**Day 3 — Neighborhoods + Depart**
- Morning bike ride on the lakefront trail
- Wicker Park or West Loop brunch
- Pilsen if time permits
- Depart from Midway or O'Hare
